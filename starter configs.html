Starter Keyboard Configurations



$100-$300


Typically a starter Keyboard is anywhere from $100-300. For 100 you will be pretty limited unless you are willing to put a lot of time and energy into it. For $150+ you should be able to get a good tray mount aluminum board with some nice switches. At that price point you will be somewhat limited in the quality of keycaps you can get, but you should be able to get excellent colors from TaiHao sets without breaking the bank.

If you have a budget of $300-600 you should be able to upgrade yourself to a top or gasket mount and some nicer swtiches and higher quality keycaps. If you are silling to wait on a Group Buy, many high quality keycaps will start at around $100 for a base set though it could take a year to get to your door. Your best bet for keyboard at this price will be catching a drop on kbdfans or getting someone for a good price on the mech market.

$600+


If you are fully committed to the keyboard hobby and want to get yourself something really nice, consider joining Group Buys. All high end keyboards are sold in limited quentities in a group buy. You will be paying 300-500 or more upfront and waiting 6-10 months but it will be worth it. ALong with your fancy aluminum annodized gasket mount board, you can pick up some smooth linear or tactile switches. You will, of course, want to lube them to get the smoothest possible experience. You should also join a group buy for your favorite GMK keycaps. By joining a group buy you can avoid the scalpers and overall increases resale prices of the mech market. Buying GMK keycaps second hand can cost you 2x or even 3x what you might have paid in the group buy.
